Regulatory Requirements

Tax preparation and filing services in Canada are governed by the Income Tax Act (R.S.C., 1985, c. 1 (5th Supp.)). Our CPAs are licensed under CPA Canada and are authorized to prepare and file returns and to represent clients before the Canada Revenue Agency.

To act as your authorized representative with the CRA, we require a signed authorization form (T1013 for individuals, RC59 for corporations). All tax positions taken in returns are supported by the Income Tax Act, CRA administrative positions, or documented professional judgement.

Required Forms
CRA T1013 (individuals)
CRA RC59 (corporations)
T183 / T183CORP
Key Filing Deadlines
  • T1 personal return: April 30 (June 15 for self-employed; balance owing still due April 30)
  • T2 corporate return: 6 months after fiscal year-end
  • T4 / T4A slips: February 28
  • Instalment payments: March 15, June 15, September 15, December 15 (personal)

Regulatory Requirements

Payroll processing in Canada must comply with the Canada Pension Plan Act, the Employment Insurance Act, and applicable provincial employment standards legislation. We process remittances in accordance with CRA requirements and file T4 and T4A information returns annually by the February 28 deadline.

Contractor classification (employee vs. independent contractor) carries regulatory and tax implications. Where there is ambiguity, we will flag this for review before onboarding a worker under contractor arrangements.

Note: Payroll remittances are due on a schedule set by CRA based on your average monthly withholding. Late remittances attract penalties and interest. We will advise you of your remittance schedule and deadlines as part of onboarding.

04
GST/HST/PST Services
Tax Compliance

GST/HST/PST returns prepared and filed on time, every period. We track filing periods, review input tax credits, and handle catch-up or correction of overdue filings.

GST/HST PST ITC Review Catch-Up
What Is Included
  • GST/HST return preparation and filing (monthly, quarterly, or annual)
  • PST return preparation and filing where applicable
  • Filing-period tracking and deadline management
  • Input tax credit (ITC) review and optimization
  • Catch-up and correction of overdue or unfiled returns
  • CRA GST/HST account status review
  • Voluntary disclosure support for late or unfiled periods
Regulatory Requirements

GST/HST is governed by the Excise Tax Act (R.S.C., 1985, c. E-15). Businesses with annual taxable supplies exceeding $30,000 are required to register for GST/HST. Filing frequency (monthly, quarterly, or annual) is determined by CRA based on annual taxable revenue. Late-filed returns attract penalties and interest.

PST requirements vary by province. In Ontario, there is no separate PST — HST applies. In provinces such as British Columbia, Manitoba, and Saskatchewan, PST is administered provincially and separate registration and filing obligations apply.

Required Documentation
Engagement Letter
CRA RC59 (to access GST/HST account)

Note: If you have unfiled GST/HST returns, do not wait. Penalties compound and CRA may assess an estimated amount on your behalf. We handle catch-up filings and can advise on voluntary disclosure where appropriate.
