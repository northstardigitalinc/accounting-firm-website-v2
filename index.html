Regulatory Requirements

Investment advisory and portfolio management services in Canada are regulated under provincial securities legislation. In Ontario, advisors must be registered with the Ontario Securities Commission (OSC) under the Securities Act, R.S.O. 1990, c. S.5. Registration categories applicable to our services include Portfolio Manager (PM) and/or Exempt Market Dealer (EMD), depending on the nature of products recommended.

We comply with National Instrument 31-103 (Registration Requirements, Exemptions and Ongoing Registrant Obligations), which governs know-your-client (KYC), know-your-product (KYP), and suitability obligations. All investment recommendations are made only after completing a full KYC assessment and determining suitability for the client's specific financial situation, risk tolerance, and investment objectives.

We are also subject to FINTRAC (Financial Transactions and Reports Analysis Centre of Canada) reporting obligations under the Proceeds of Crime (Money Laundering) and Terrorist Financing Act. This requires identity verification at account opening and ongoing transaction monitoring.

Regulatory Requirements

Audits of financial statements in Canada must be conducted by a public accountant licensed under the applicable provincial public accounting act (e.g., the Public Accounting Act, 2004 in Ontario). We do not perform statutory audits. Our role is to prepare your organization and act as liaison, with the audit itself conducted by a licensed public accounting firm.

Our compliance services are tailored to the regulatory requirements applicable to your industry. For federally regulated financial institutions, this includes OSFI guidelines and the Bank Act or Trust and Loan Companies Act. For healthcare organizations, this includes provincial health funding accountability requirements and applicable college regulations. For publicly reporting issuers, this includes securities law financial reporting requirements under NI 52-109 and NI 52-110.

All internal control frameworks are designed in accordance with COSO (Committee of Sponsoring Organizations of the Treadway Commission) Internal Control Integrated Framework, which is the standard recognized by Canadian auditors and regulators.
